Game evaluation

Positive reviews

On Metacritic, a special site for American review, 57 well-known critics from various media commented. Only one scored 60 points (out of 100), 4 scored more than 80 points, 9 scored full points, and others generally praised more than 90 points.

Mike Laidlaw, the chief designer of "Dragon Age: Origins" in 2009, believes that online game production must consider the impact of World of Warcraft on players. It is unwise to ignore World of Warcraft's share and influence in the online game market. Just like "Quaker" set the basic framework for FPS games, World of Warcraft also set the most common user interface for MMO games.

In China, online games are criticized and questioned rather than admired, especially with CCTV as the mainstream official media. But in the video on CCTV-13 channel in 2011, the host affirmed the online game "World of Warcraft" for the first time, and praised its creativity and innovation, which is in sharp contrast with domestic products.

The 2012 American Online Game Developers Conference Jury praised: "World of Warcraft" has not only greatly promoted the development of the online game industry, but has also been popular in the past eight years of operation. There are still users from all over the world who are still active. Join. The upcoming new expansion is expected to create a new record for the game, making this brand increasingly prominent as an important symbol of contemporary popular culture.

negative comment

In 2009, Tao Hongkai publicly stated in a CCTV program that World of Warcraft is a "drug" and criticized Warcraft for making young people addicted to the Internet. Then in 2012, Tao Hongkai became the chief game appraiser of "Bright Sword 2". And in the connection with the TV column Game Fengyun Tao Hongkai said: "In terms of content, this (Liangjian 2) is many times better than such games as "Warcraft" and "Legend."

At the 2007 Montreal Game Show in Canada, the winner of the IGF Design Innovation Award Jonathan Brow said that "World of Warcraft" is like a drug, and its rules are the meaning of a fool's life.

In 2013, the designer of the League of Legends, Morello, said that World of Warcraft blindly caters to players, but never realizes that players' complaints on the forum are not what they really want to change.
